Victorian Compliance Rules

Navigating the regulatory landscape is essential for a legal and safe renovation. In Victoria, building permits are required for any structural changes, the relocation of wet areas, or new plumbing installations. Simple cosmetic updates, such as painting or replacing fixtures in the same position, generally do not require a permit. However, homeowners must ensure that all domestic building work valued over $10,000 is performed by a registered building practitioner. This credential verifies that the builder has the necessary qualifications and insurance to undertake the work.

Specific trades require their own licensing; plumbing must be performed by VBA-registered plumbers, and electrical work must be carried out by electricians licensed by Energy Safe Victoria. Financial protections are also mandated by law. Domestic Building Insurance is compulsory for any project exceeding $16,000, providing cover if the builder cannot complete the work. Furthermore, all waterproofing in wet areas must strictly comply with the Australian Standard AS 3740:2021 to prevent long-term structural damage.

Heritage and Period Homes

Camberwell is renowned for its Federation and Edwardian architecture, which presents specific challenges for renovations. These period homes often contain lath-and-plaster walls, original floor levels, narrow service runs, and decorative detailing that complicate modern installations. A standard renovation approach can damage these character features, so a heritage-sympathetic design is necessary to maintain the property's aesthetic integrity while introducing modern amenities.

Renovating a heritage bathroom frequently involves additional costs. Homeowners should budget for the careful relocation of plumbing to avoid disturbing existing structures and the selection of tile profiles and fixtures that complement the era. Practical implications include allowing extra time for these complex tasks and preparing for unknowns that older substrates often reveal once demolition begins. Common questions

Do I need a building permit for a bathroom update? Yes, a building permit is required in Victoria if the work involves structural changes, relocating the wet area, or new plumbing. Cosmetic updates like painting or replacing a vanity in the same location usually do not require a permit.

What is the biggest cost factor in a renovation? Labour is the most significant cost, typically accounting for 40 to 60 per cent of the total budget. In Camberwell, costs can also rise due to the complexity of plumbing relocations in older Federation or Edwardian homes.

What insurance is required for my project? Domestic Building Insurance is mandatory for all domestic building work valued over $16,000. This insurance protects the homeowner if the builder goes bankrupt, disappears, or loses their registration.
